Showcase: Sammy's.

The goal was to design a high-fidelity website design scaled effectively for various devices including mobile, tablet, and desktop. This was to be done using Figma or Adobe XD as was taught in BCITâ€™s New Media and Design program. It was also to be done in a manner to allow for effecient development afterwards.

For this project, I selected a restaurant-based brand that met the project requirements of having a unique menu for mobile/desktop platforms, a gallery, and showcasing skills developed in class. I conducted research on other restaurant websites to gather ideas on content and structure, and also created a brand with colors, fonts, and logo that were appealing and down-to-earth. The color scheme primarily consisted of orange and white, with the logo having a simple handwriting style to create an authentic presentation. The fonts used were Bebas Neue for headlines and Baskerville for the menu.

I designed the layout with grids to make it easily convertible to a web page by a developer. The "mobile first" approach was used, with a 4-column grid for mobile and 12-column grids for desktop and tablet devices to ensure consistency across devices. For example, a map and location information section took up all 4 columns on mobile, while text was placed below it. The design was reorganized across more columns as it was scaled up for larger screens. The desktop showcased 4 navigational items, while the mobile design used a hamburger menu to contain them. The layout was easy to implement due to the grid's easy content placement, and figma sizes transferred smoothly to establish breakpoints for responsive designs.

Made using Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator.
